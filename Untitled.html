Let's think step by step. What is electroencephalography?

Electroencephalography (EEG) is a non-invasive neuroimaging technique that measures the electrical activity of the brain. EEG recordings are typically made by placing electrodes on the scalp, which detect the electrical impulses produced by the brain's neurons. These impulses are then analyzed to provide information about the brain's activity, including its rhythms, patterns, and responses to stimuli.
EEG is commonly used in a variety of applications, including:
1. Diagnosis and monitoring of neurological disorders: EEG can help diagnose and monitor conditions such as epilepsy, seizures, and encephalitis.
2. Sleep disorders: EEG can be used to diagnose and monitor sleep disorders such as insomnia, narcolepsy, and sleep apnea.
3. Brain function: EEG can be used to study the brain's electrical activity in response to various stimuli, such as drugs, alcohol, and sensory deprivation.
4. Brain-computer interfaces: EEG can be used to develop brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) that allow people to control devices with their thoughts.
5. Neurofeedback training: EEG can be used for neurofeedback training, which involves using the brain's electrical activity to teach self-regulation of brain function.
